Dead or Alive 2 Ayane Notes

Hey, I can host stuff like this on my own site and format it however I want! Neat. Anyway, here’s the results of some cursory labwork with Ayane in Dead or Alive 2. Use them how you please.

Neutral Tools

3P: safe mid, good range + pushback, crit stun on crouch hit, 4P follow up risky mid CH launch vs abare

66P: advancing safe mid launcher, tech crouch, good reward on crouch/counter hit

33P: safe advancing low, 2K follow up low crit stun on hit

3K: good, simple mid kick for poking, good pushback on block, crit stun on crouch hit, sit stun on CH

9K: gimmicky approach option, safe but minus, kd on hit

236K: super sick spinning dropkick, safe when spaced

4F+K: long range spin kick, limbo stun on hit in most situations, guaranteed follow-ups

Pressure Tools

PP: H>H, safe + jail on block, P extension safe mid launch, mix with KK extension to check H-hold

6PP: both hits unsafe, second hit checks abare with CH launch

1P: safe tech crouch low, enters BT, [1]K counter hits abare attempts, true combo on CH

1K: safe standing low, general good low check

6K: fast mid kick, unsafe but crit stun on hit, 6KK oB beats P but loses to 2P, 2K beats 2P but loses to P

BT Tools

BT PP[1]PK: BT PP string into [1]PK, tech crouch and CH 2P abare, use to force respect

BT PP6P: BT PP string into mid, small crit stun on crouch hit, combos to 2K follow-up

BT 2P: Tech crouch mid, crit stun on hit, launch on CH, unsafe on block

BT [1]P: identical to [1]P, can lead to looping pressure

3~4, 3~1, [3]: tricky movement out of BT. fairly safe due to tech crouch, use to make people hesitate or to throw off potential hold attempts

Throws

64F+P: no damage sideswap, but nets you a guaranteed(?) launch with 4P

66F+P 8F+P: combo throw, most dmg but follow up is breakable

214F+P: most damaging standalone throw

46F+P: extra dmg when back to wall

BT F+P: throw option from BT

Holds

Most rewarding hold is mid kick hold, since it leads to a juggle. Wary opponents may elect to use mid punches during crit stun sequences, or even just not use mids altogether. P/K distinction matters slightly less if you’re allowing Dreamcast control scheme because 3-way holds lmao.

Launchers

66P: low launch on NH, higher launch on crouch/counter hit

4P: launches on CH/stun

8K: stuns on NH, launches on CH/stun

BT 2P: as above in BT Tools section

BT 7P: similar to BT 2P, but more useful in stun sequences

Juggles

4P (low launch vs heavies, enters BT for mixups)

4P2K (low launch vs lights/mids)

4P.K (high launch vs most characters)

P+KPP7K (high launch vs most characters)

BT PP6P.K (high launch vs most characters from BT)

BT PP4PP7K (high launch vs most characters from BT)

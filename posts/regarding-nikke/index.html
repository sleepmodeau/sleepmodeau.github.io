Regarding the NIKKE Piece

A short while ago I wrote a first impressions piece on the recently-released mobile game Goddess of Victory: NIKKE, where I blasted it for largely being “just another JRPG focused on DPS checks” rather than actually taking advantage of its core mechanical premise of being a Time Crisis-esque cover shooter. This involved a rundown of some of the game’s core mechanics that was, to put it mildly, extremely fucking wrong.

Of course, a first impression is gonna get some things wrong to an extent, but a lot of the mechanics I described did not actually work in the way that I insinuated, or they were far more impactful than I gave them credit for, and the result was a piece which, looking back, I am not particularly proud of.

My headspace when I wrote the NIKKE piece was basically one of “people said this game was kinda like this one thing that I like, but it kinda wasn’t really”, and given that I’m the kind of person that usually doesn’t like mobage anyway, in retrospect it’s pretty clear to see that such a headspace is not particularly condusive to writing anything with a degree of intellectual honesty, let alone factual accuracy.1

So the piece is gone now. I probably won’t keep this one up forever, but I felt it pertinent to exercise a bit of honesty here. This retraction isn’t the result of me playing the game and realising that I was wrong, because I still don’t like the game and I’m not going to play it again. So you likely won’t see me talking about it again, either.

So… yeah. My bad.

  1. When people who actually play the game, and readily admit that it sucks, are telling you that you are extremely wrong, then you know you fucked up ↩︎
